How do I know if my car is affected?

If you are currently using mBrace, you're affected. The service ends permanently on January 1, 2026 with no replacement from Mercedes. Affected vehicles include: 2017 C-Class Coupe, GLS, AMG GT, S-Class Cabriolet / 2016 C-Class, E-Class, CLS, M-Class/GLE, SL, GL, SLK/SLC, G-Class, B-Class, CLA, S-Class, GLC, GLE Coupe / 2015–2016 AMG GT.

How does the SmartKey Starter remote start work?

Out of the box, it works via your OEM factory key fob. If your key has a panic button, press it to remote start. If not, use a lock–unlock–lock sequence. No new key fobs or hardware required to get started.

Can I control my car from my phone?

Yes. The Compustar Drone module adds full smartphone control — remote start, stop, lock, unlock, GPS tracking, vehicle immobilization, and alarm notifications. It's plug-and-play with the SmartKey Starter.

Is there a subscription fee?

Key fob remote start has no subscription required. Phone control via Compustar Drone requires a DroneMobile app subscription — significantly less expensive than mBrace, with annual and multi-year discounts available. See all plans at dronemobile.com/plans.

How long does the remote start run?

Default run time is 15 minutes for passenger vehicles and 1 hour for Sprinter and Metris. Both are easily configurable through the instrument cluster menu.

Will the car shut off when I open the door?

No. When remotely started, the vehicle stays running when you open the door. You also retain full Keyless Go functionality (lock and unlock from the door handle) while remotely started.

Is there a limit on the number of remote starts?

DAS4 vehicles have no limit. DAS3 vehicles allow 5 remote starts before requiring a drive cycle. This is a significant improvement over the mBrace factory limit of 2 starts at a time.

I already lost connectivity with the 3G shutdown — can I still get this?

Absolutely. Our SmartKey Starter is compatible with most Mercedes-Benz vehicles going back to 2008. The mBrace shutdown doesn't change your eligibility.
